Bealby Ploughs

In the 70’s I ask my father to see if he could locate an original Bealby plough. He contacted a gentleman called Charlie Sheldon (I believe Jane’s husband was related?).

Charlie Shelton had two and sold one to my Dad for £30. I have two sons, so a year later I asked Dad to see if he could buy the other. Charlie Sheldon agreed and also to the “same price as last time”. I sent him a cheque, which was duly returned as he wanted cash not a cheque!! My father told me that when he was with Charlie Sheldon fixing the “deal”, he phoned his son to say what he had done. I believe his son was most unhappy with what had been done, but Charlie apparently said that the deal had been done and that was that.

My father told me that his father had a number of “Match Ploughs” which were used in competitions. I believe that when the brothers came home during WW2 on leave, they burnt the ploughs on the fire due to wood shortages.
